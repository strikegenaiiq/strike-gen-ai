# STRIKE GEN AI — Business Model

Version: 0.1

Date: 2026-07-09

Author: STRIKE GEN AI Strategy Team

---

## 1. Executive Summary

STRIKE GEN AI is a creator-focused AI platform that enables individuals, creators, agencies, and enterprises to generate professional videos, images, and audio from prompts. This business model documents the strategic approach to product-market fit, monetization, partnerships, and growth. It outlines pricing options, the credit economy, customer segments, acquisition and retention strategies, and risks with mitigations.

---

## 2. Vision and Mission Alignment

Vision: To democratize AI-powered content creation by providing accessible, scalable, and intelligent tools that turn ideas into professional-quality digital assets.

Mission: To build an intuitive creator platform that simplifies content production with automation, intelligent workflows, and affordable access to advanced creative technologies.

This business model aligns monetization and product features with the mission by offering tiered plans, pay-as-you-go credits, and enterprise options that scale with customer needs.

---

## 3. Value Proposition

- Fast creation: Convert prompts into finished media faster than traditional pipelines.
- Cost efficiency: Reduce production costs through automated AI generation.
- Unified workflow: Manage projects, assets, billing, and teams in a single platform.
- Quality controls: Offer styling, resolution, and post-processing controls to meet professional standards.
- Scalable usage: Support one-off creators up to enterprise volume customers with predictable billing and credit management.

## 6. Credit System

The credit system monetizes compute and provider costs into a simple unit that customers consume when generating media.

Core rules (planning-stage):
- Each generation operation is estimated in credits prior to execution; final cost is reconciled after job completion.
- Credits can be included as part of subscriptions (monthly allotments) or purchased as top-ups.
- Credits may expire based on plan rules (e.g., monthly allotments reset on renewal).
- Admin/finance can issue promotional credits or adjustments for refunds and support.

Credit types:
- Allocated credits — given by subscription renewals or promotions.
- Purchased credits — bought via payment flow.
- Reserved credits — reserved at job submission to ensure coverage.

## 7. Pricing Strategy

Guiding principles:
- Provide clear, transparent pricing tied to credit consumption.
- Offer low-friction entry (free tier) to encourage trial and conversion.
- Use tiered plans to surface value and create upgrade paths.
- Allow pay-as-you-go for irregular users and enterprise billing for large customers.

Pricing table (example — planning-stage illustrative tiers)

| Plan | Monthly Price | Included Credits | Target Customer |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Free | $0 | 10 credits (trial) | New users, testers
| Creator | $19 | 200 credits | Casual creators, social media
| Pro | $99 | 1,500 credits | Frequent creators, small agencies
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom allocation | Large teams, high volume

Notes:
- Per-generation credit costs depend on provider cost, duration, resolution, and media type.
- Discounts for annual billing, volume purchases, and committed-use enterprise agreements are expected.

## 8. Cost Structure

Primary cost centers:
- Third-party AI provider usage fees (per generation cost)
- Cloud infrastructure (storage, compute, CDN)
- Payments and transaction fees
- Personnel (engineering, SRE, product, sales, support)
- Marketing and customer acquisition costs
- Compliance, security, and legal costs

Cost model notes:
- The variable cost per generated asset is primarily driven by external AI provider pricing; margin management requires close monitoring of per-job economics and pricing adjustments.
- Storage costs for media assets grow with user adoption—lifecycle policies and tiered storage reduce expense.

## 16. Growth Strategy

Short-term (0–12 months):
- Focus on product-market fit for individual creators and small agencies.
- Launch freemium model with trial credits and starter templates.
- Build referral and content marketing channels.

Medium-term (12–24 months):
- Optimize unit economics and introduce Pro tier features.
- Expand provider integrations to improve cost/quality trade-offs.
- Begin targeting mid-market agencies with team and billing features.

Long-term (24+ months):
- Enterprise sales motion with compliance, SLAs, and dedicated support.
- Marketplace for templates, models, and professional services.
- Developer API and partner ecosystem.

## 17. Financial Assumptions

Note: This section lists planning assumptions for unit economics without producing numerical forecasts tied to unknown future data.

Key assumptions (examples for planning):
- Per-generation cost varies by media type and provider; estimate and monitor average cost per credit.
- Conversion rates from free to paid plans depend on onboarding quality and trial design.
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) will vary by channel; monitor payback period and LTV/CAC ratios.

Financial hygiene:
- Track per-job profitability, aggregated by plan and customer segment.
- Monitor churn, average revenue per user (ARPU), and usage per cohort.

---

## 18. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Operational and business KPIs to monitor:
- Monthly active users (MAU) and daily active users (DAU)
- Number of generated assets (volume) and generation throughput
- Credit consumption rate and average credits per user
- Conversion rate (free -> paid) and churn rate
- ARPU and MRR/ARR
- CAC and LTV
- Payment success rate and refund rate
- Infrastructure cost per generation and gross margin per generation

## 19. Risks and Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---:|---|
| Provider cost volatility | High | Multi-provider strategy, dynamic routing, cost controls, and surge pricing/policies |
| Abuse of platform (policy violations) | Medium-High | Rate limits, moderation tools, automated content detection, reporting workflows |
| Payment fraud and chargebacks | Medium | Use reputable payment providers, fraud detection, clear billing records |
| Rapid storage growth | Medium | Lifecycle policies, tiered storage, user controls for retention |
| Poor product-market fit | High | Early experiments, close user feedback loops, iterative product development |
